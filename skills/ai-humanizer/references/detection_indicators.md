# AI Detection Indicators

## Lexical Indicators: Vocabulary and Phrasing "Tells"

### Lower Lexical Diversity and Token Selection
LLMs favor predictable, high-probability tokens. Human writers use visceral, low-frequency language to create impact.

**Examples:**
- AI: "The team worked hard to improve the product for the users."
- Human: "The team gutted the interface to fix the friction points users were screaming about."

### Punctuation Patterns

### Absence of Idioms
AIGT lacks natural, idiomatic expressions and metaphors. It is overly literal, missing cultural nuances.

**Examples:**
- AI: "This task is very easy to finish."
- Human: "This task is a total cakewalk."

## Grammatical & Syntactic Fingerprints

### The Formal/Impersonal Bias
AI over-relies on specific parts of speech that create a "cold" and bureaucratic tone.

**Key Patterns:**

| Feature              | AIGT Tendency                                                                   | Impact                                                 |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Parts of Speech      | Increased Nouns, Determiners, Adpositions; lower Adjectives and Adverbs         | Creates "heavy," information-dense text lacking nuance |
| Nominalization       | Converts verbs/adjectives to noun phrases                                       | Makes prose static and bureaucratic                    |
| Syntactic Complexity | Higher dependency distances                                                     | Grammatically correct but taxes working memory         |
| Punctuation          | Dominance of commas/periods; absence of dashes, parentheses, semicolons, colons | Rhythmic monotony                                      |

### Nominalization Examples
**AI:** "The utilization of this methodology led to an acceleration in the provision of services."
**Human:** "Using this method helped us provide services faster."

## Sentence Architecture Patterns

### Low Burstiness and Uniform Rhythm
Sentences cluster around narrow band of length and complexity, lacking alternation between short and long structures.

### Subject Concentration
Complex ideas packaged into simple declarative frames: "The logic is: X" or "The result is: Y"

### The "Subject+Verb+Object, Present Participle" Formula
Attaching "-ing" phrases to sentence ends for elaboration: "The system optimizes the workflow, reducing the time required..."

### Avoidance of Basic Copulatives
Substituting simple verbs (is/are) with formal constructions: "serves as," "stands as," "marks," "represents"

### Formulaic Scaffolding
Symmetric section lengths, rhetorical Q&A structures, bulleted lists separated by colons
